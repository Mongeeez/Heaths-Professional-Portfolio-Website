About Me

Hi, I'm Heath Lanmon, a 15-year-old aspiring software developer with a passion for learning and community service. Over the past year, I had the incredible opportunity to be a part of the Air Force Junior ROTC (AFJROTC). This experience has taught me valuable leadership skills, discipline, and the importance of teamwork. In addition to my time in AFJROTC, I have dedicated numerous hours to the California Highway Patrol (CHP) Explorer Program. Through this program, I've had the chance to help out with various community events and proudly embody the role of their mascot, Chipper, whenever needed(I also got to make a custom decal in Rocket League for the CHP:). These experiences have not only strengthened my sense of community but also allowed me to give back in meaningful ways. Looking ahead, I am thrilled to share that in the summer of 2025, I will be attending the Aim High Flight Academy offered by the Air Force. This exciting opportunity will allow me to learn how to fly planes and further explore my passion for aviation. Combining my technical skills with my dedication to service and leadership, I am eager to continue growing and making a positive impact both in my community and in the field of technology.
